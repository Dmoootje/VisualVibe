# One-page versus full website English localisation review

## Scope and outcome

Independently reviewed the complete Dutch source, English translation and localisation brief. Approved without substantive corrections.

## Parity and quality

- Source: 2,804 words; translation: 2,979 words.
- All 10 H2 sections and all 7 FAQs are preserved.
- Exact component parity: 1 LeadIntro, 1 ComparisonTable, 2 FeatureGrids, 2 ChecklistBlocks, 1 NoticeBox, 1 DoDontGrid, 1 RoadmapBlock, 1 QuoteBlock and 1 BlogCTA.
- The commercial decision framework remains balanced: neither format is presented as inherently cheap, professional, fast, scalable or better for SEO.
- Audience, offer complexity, separate search intents, evidence, conversion paths, content growth and maintenance remain the actual decision criteria.

## Claims and safeguards

- The article does not claim that Google penalises one-page websites as a format.
- It clearly identifies the inference that multiple meaningful routes create more opportunities for precise topic and internal-link relationships.
- Anchor links are not presented as substitutes for standalone landing pages.
- Price is tied to scope, strategy, content, design, media, integrations and responsibility rather than page count alone.
- Core Web Vitals are described as one part of page experience and ranking, not a guarantee.
- Accessibility, analytics, redirects and future expansion are retained as project considerations.

## Technical review

Confirmed production public routes under `/en/kennisbank/` and `/en/services/`, canonical internal relationship identities, natural UK English, complete SEO/GEO metadata, a matching localisation brief and clean route, encoding and long-dash scans.
