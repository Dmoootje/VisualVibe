# Real estate photography tips: an honest, complete property story

<LeadIntro>
Good **real estate photography** helps prospective buyers or tenants understand a property's layout, how its spaces connect and which finishes are genuinely present. It uses light, viewpoint and styling to provide clarity, but should not make a room appear larger, brighter or more luxurious than it proves to be during a viewing.
</LeadIntro>

This requires preparation from the owner, broker and photographer. Loose items, defective lamps or an unclear shot sequence cost time on location. Too extreme a wide angle, heavy image processing or random selection can then distort expectations. With a fixed working method, the report becomes more complete, more consistent and easier to publish.

Professional photos can enhance the presentation of a sales, rental or project page. They do not guarantee a faster transaction, higher price or more requests. Location, condition, asking price, market, information quality and follow-up also play a role. This guide therefore discusses what you can organize and control.

<QuoteBlock>
A property photograph should create interest while remaining faithful to reality.
</QuoteBlock>

## First determine what the photos are for

An apartment for sale requires a different emphasis than a new construction project in progress, a holiday home or an office that is rented out. Before the shoot, write down the primary goal, target audience and publication channels. Also note which characteristics are decisive: outdoor space, light, circulation, renovation quality, visibility from the street or technical facilities.

Distinguish between a real estate listing and a broader architecture or realization report. With a real estate listing, a candidate must be able to assess the entire offer. An architectural report can focus more on material, concept and detail. One production can support both, but only if the shot list mentions both goals.

## Prepare the building without making it unrecognizable

Preparation is not an attempt to erase all traces of use. It removes visual noise, makes functions clear and prevents privacy problems. A home may feel occupied, but personal documents, family photos, medication, keys, mail and screen information do not belong in a public advertisement.

Walk through the building one or two days in advance. On the morning itself, a defective lamp, full waste bin or wet terrace prove to be more difficult to solve. Agree who will manage cleaning, styling, access and final checks. The photographer can move small elements for composition, but a full clean-up or moving job rarely fits into the shooting time.

## Build a shot list that follows the tour

A full series feels like a visit. Start at the location or front facade, go through the entrance to the main living space and then follow the real circulation. Show bedrooms, sanitary facilities, functional areas and outdoor space in a logical order. A candidate does not have to guess for themselves whether two photos show the same room.

Prioritize one explanatory overview per room. Add a second view when it shows a different passage, layout or asset. Details are useful for material and finish, but should not crowd out essential spaces. An advertisement with ten close-ups and no full view of the bedroom is visually rich but informationally poor.

In addition, make a list of images that should not be published. Consider security codes, key numbers, valuables, recognizable children, art collections or technical details that could pose a security risk. Don't just decide this during the online selection.

## Plan light per facade and space

Daylight determines how an interior reads. A window on the east side receives different light early than a west-facing living space. A terrace can be completely in the shade at the wrong time. Note orientation and priority outdoor images when planning and take season and weather forecast into account.

Cloudy weather provides soft, even light and can be suitable for interiors. Sun gives direction and liveliness, but also harsh contrasts. The goal is not to whiten every window or remove every shadow. Post-processing can control dynamic range as long as window views, lights and surfaces remain believable.

Work consistently per room. Mixed color temperatures of daylight, warm lamps and cool LED lighting can color white walls differently. Turn off disturbing sources or tune lights in advance. Correct local color cast in moderation; Making an entire room neutral at the expense of materials and atmosphere is also not correct.

## Use perspective to explain space

A wide-angle lens can completely show a small space, but an extremely wide angle of view stretches edges and makes distances greater. Choose the point of view and focal point that will keep the layout readable. Keep camera and vertical lines as straight as possible. Correct perspective carefully, without unnaturally distorting furniture and doorways.

Usually shoot from a stable height that suits the space. Too high mainly shows floor; too low leaves tables and beds blocking the room. The ideal height is not the same everywhere. In a kitchen, worktop and cabinet lines are decisive, in a bathroom you want to avoid mirror reflections and in a high entrance, architecture should be given more space.

A door frame or piece of furniture in the foreground can add depth, but should not obscure a large part of the image. Show connection points: an open door to the hall, the relationship between kitchen and dining area, or the route to the terrace. This way, someone not only understands what a room looks like, but also where it is located.

## Show features and limitations without visual tricks

A good presentation chooses the best realistic moment and point of view. That is different from hiding defects, dimensions or surroundings. Remove a dust spot or temporary cable in consultation, but do not change any permanent construction, appearance, material or permanent damage without a clear process and permission.

Include exact areas, orientation, energy information and technical characteristics as controlled text fields in the property listing. Don't deduce them from the photo. Where relevant, add a plan that clarifies the connection between spaces. Photography shows experience and finish; a plan and correct data show structure and measure.

Let styling support function. A dining table with four chairs makes use understandable. A bed that does not really fit in the room, a digitally enlarged garden or virtually added luxury interior can create the wrong expectation. When using virtual staging, label those images clearly and also keep realistic images of the current state.

## Check privacy and right to image

An interior photo may contain more personal data than is visible at first glance. Family photos, names on post, diplomas, agendas, license plates and information on screens can identify people. Before publishing, zoom in on each selected photo and check mirrors, windows, refrigerator doors, noticeboards and documents.

If residents, employees, neighbors or visitors are recognizable in the picture, specific agreements are necessary. The Belgian Data Protection Authority explains in the [principles of the right to image](https://gegevensbeschermingsautoriteit.be/burger/thema-s/recht-op-afbeelding/principes) that consent to make an image and consent to publication are assessed separately. Avoid random people where possible and establish purpose and channels when recognisability is necessary.

The photography itself may also be protected by copyright. The [FAQ Copyright of FPS Economy](https://economie.fgov.be/sites/default/files/Files/Publications/files/FAQ-auteursrecht.pdf) provides general information about visual works and use. Agree with the photographer, broker and client who can publish, edit, pass on and reuse the photos. Check separately whether artworks, designs or brand expressions require extra attention in a specific campaign. This article is not legal advice.

<NoticeBox variant="warning" title="Publish only after a privacy round">
An illegible laptop-sized document may be readable on the master file. Therefore, check the high resolution, not just the small web preview.
</NoticeBox>

## Only combine photography with media that add something

A 3D tour helps when the spatial route is important and candidates want to look around independently. Read [what a 3D tour is](/en/kennisbank/3d-vr/what-is-a-3d-tour/) to review preparation, privacy and placement separately. Drone images can clarify the location, plot or project context; the pillar about [drone images for companies](/en/kennisbank/drone/drone-footage-for-businesses/) covers feasibility, safety and briefing.

Not every apartment needs all formats. Choose photography as a clear, quickly scannable basis. Add a plan, video, 3D or drone when this solves a question that the photo series cannot properly answer. Plan combined shots as one production, but give each medium its own shot priority and privacy controls.

## Select for completeness, not just beauty

The most striking photo is not always the best main image. Choose an opener that shows the most important spatial feature and remains readable even as a small map image. Then build a logical sequence. Avoid five variations of the same corner while a bedroom or storage room is missing.

Check straight lines, sharpness, color, window details, reflections and mutual consistency before delivery. Compare adjacent images: a wall should not be a different color in every photo. Keep retouching decisions traceable and store masters separately from web exports.

## Publish quickly, findable and accessible

Create a suitable format for each application. A large hero requires different dimensions than a card in a search overview. Responsive images help prevent a phone from downloading the heaviest master file. Save enough detail for a gallery or zoom, but don't automatically load dozens of large images before someone views them.

Google recommends standard HTML images, relevant text around the image, responsive variants, descriptive names and useful alt text in its [Image SEO Guidelines](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/google-images). These are conditions for an understandable implementation, not a guarantee of traffic or visibility.

Write alt text from the function on the page. "Bright living space with open kitchen and sliding door to terrace" provides relevant information. 'Best real estate photography Limburg cheap house' is a keyword list and does not help the visitor. Only mention an address or personal name when it is functional and appropriate. The [W3C alt decision tree](https://www.w3.org/WAI/tutorials/images/decision-tree/) helps determine when a short description or empty alt text is appropriate.

Connect the report with clear property details, a contact route and any local context. The guide to [local SEO for SMEs in Limburg](/en/kennisbank/seo-geo/local-seo-for-smes-in-limburg/) explains how real local information, website and company profile together form a more consistent signal.

## Evaluate presentation without sales claims

First check operational quality: is every priority space present, is the order correct, does the gallery and zoom work, are mobile cutouts good and are privacy-sensitive details nowhere online? Measure load time and errors on image URLs. Also keep track of how many correction rounds arise due to insufficient preparation.

Then you can compare page interactions: do visitors open the gallery, view plan or 3D tour, and reach the contact action? Carefully compare similar properties. Asking price, location, season, campaign budget and market demand make a direct causal relationship difficult.

Use feedback from visits. If candidates are structurally surprised by scale, light or condition, check whether the image, plan and text together give an overly rosy or unclear expectation. The goal is not as many clicks as possible, but more informed interest.

## A strong report makes the visit recognizable

The best real estate photography is not the series with the most effects. It is the series in which a candidate recognizes spaces, light and finishes upon arrival. Prepare the property carefully, give each photo an information task and check selection, privacy and publication as a whole.
