# GEO and AEO for SMEs: appearing in Google, ChatGPT and AI search results

<LeadIntro>
**SMEs improve their chances of appearing in AI search results by publishing crawlable, well-structured and factually consistent content that answers real questions, identifies the business clearly and supports claims with evidence.** GEO and AEO build on sound SEO. No setting, schema type or writing formula can guarantee a citation.
</LeadIntro>

People now move between traditional search results, AI Overviews, ChatGPT and other answer systems. That changes how information is presented, but it does not remove the need for a technically accessible website, useful content and credible evidence. For an SME, the practical goal is not to write for a mysterious algorithm. It is to make genuine expertise easy for people and systems to understand.

## GEO in one minute

Generative engine optimisation, or GEO, is the practice of making content clear and source-worthy for systems that generate answers from information they find. It concerns structure, factual precision, evidence, entities and the relationships between pages. GEO is not a guaranteed route into an answer and does not replace SEO.

## AEO in one minute

Answer engine optimisation, or AEO, focuses on making a direct answer easy to find and understand. A useful page states the question, gives a bounded answer early, and then explains conditions, examples and evidence. It serves readers first and may also help search or answer systems extract meaning.

## How do SEO, AEO and GEO fit together?

SEO provides crawlability, indexability, internal structure and relevance. AEO improves answer formats. GEO makes claims, entities and evidence easier to interpret in generative contexts. Google explicitly treats work for its generative Search features as part of SEO. It does not prescribe a separate AI schema, an ideal AI word count or a mandatory `llms.txt` file.

## Why does this matter for SMEs?

Small and medium-sized businesses rarely need to cover every topic. They need to explain their own services, audience, region, process and evidence well. A company in Limburg, Belgium, can be highly relevant for a specific local question even without the publishing volume of a national platform. Specificity and consistency create stronger signals than broad, generic marketing copy.

## How do you make content suitable for AI search?

Begin with the reader's question. Give a concise answer near the top, use headings that describe what follows, define specialist terms, and support factual claims with first-hand evidence or reliable sources. Add examples, limitations and dates where information can change. Identify the author and business, and connect the page to relevant services, regions and case studies.

## What is GEO?

The term became widely known through research into Generative Engine Optimization, but industry use is broader and still developing. In practice, GEO combines editorial clarity, technical access, evidence and entity consistency. Treat claims about platform behaviour cautiously and check current first-party documentation.

## Entities: state clearly who you are and what you do

An entity is an identifiable person, organisation, place, service or concept. Use the same business name, service terms, contact details and geographic descriptions across the website. Explain relationships directly: VisualVibe is a creative media agency serving SMEs from Limburg, Belgium, with web design, SEO, photography, video, drone, immersive media and podcasting services.

## Structured data and schema

Structured data can clarify page type, organisation, author, breadcrumbs, articles, services and videos when it matches visible content. It does not create an AI citation entitlement. Follow Google's general structured data guidelines, use the most specific valid type and never mark up claims or FAQs that visitors cannot see.

## Crawler access: Search is not the same as AI training

Check `robots.txt`, page robots directives, canonical tags, authentication and firewall rules separately. For Google's generative Search features, pages must be indexed and eligible for a search result snippet. Google lists no additional technical admission requirement that guarantees inclusion.

OpenAI distinguishes between OAI-SearchBot, which supports discovery for ChatGPT search features, and GPTBot, which concerns content that may be used to train generative models. Access can be managed separately. Document the business and privacy decision for each crawler.

<NoticeBox variant="info" title="Access is necessary, never sufficient">
A crawlable page may be eligible to be found or cited. No crawler setting guarantees a position, citation, click or lead. Measure technical access, content quality and outcomes separately.
</NoticeBox>

## How do you measure visibility in generative results?

Avoid turning an invented GEO score into the end goal. Combine visibility, source referrals and commercial outcomes. As of July 2026, reporting differs by platform and may continue to change. Google's dedicated generative AI performance report is being rolled out to some Search Console properties. Other AI-feature activity may remain in general Search reporting. Bing AI Performance reports early signals such as citation counts, cited pages and grounding queries. OpenAI states that referrals from ChatGPT Search use `utm_source=chatgpt.com`.

## Content that AI can understand more easily

Replace empty promises with concrete descriptions. "We offer complete solutions" says little. "We build websites for SMEs in Limburg with SEO, original photography and clear enquiry forms" identifies the service, audience, region and deliverable. Follow the same people-first quality principles used for conventional search.

## The role of photography and video in GEO

Original images, project footage and case studies add context and evidence. Give images meaningful alt text, explain what they show and use captions where useful. Give videos a clear title, summary or transcript, relevant thumbnail and `VideoObject` markup where appropriate. Media needs surrounding text because systems may not interpret every visual detail as a person would.

## Internal links connect your expertise

A useful knowledge base is not a set of isolated articles. Natural internal links show how an explanation relates to a service, region, case study or deeper guide. Avoid generic anchors such as "click here" and do not create links to pages that do not exist in the same locale.

## Practical GEO and AEO checklist

Check the direct answer, heading hierarchy, examples, terminology, authorship, organisation details, metadata, internal links, structured data, mobile performance and evidence. Keep names and claims consistent across pages. Use local context only where it is genuinely relevant.

## What can SMEs do now?

Make services concrete, state the real service area, answer questions customers actually ask, show evidence through case studies and media, build meaningful internal links, and add structured data that matches visible content. You do not need a perfect AI strategy before improving these fundamentals.

## Conclusion: AI visibility starts with clarity

GEO and AEO sound new, but the foundation is practical. Explain who you are, what you do, whom you serve, where you work and what supports your claims. Combine useful content and genuine evidence with crawlability, internal structure and accurate metadata.

AI changes how people search, but not what they need: clarity, trust and proof.
