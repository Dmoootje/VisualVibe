# 3D tour for real estate: from online exploration to viewing

<LeadIntro>
**A 3D real estate tour is an interactive display that allows prospective buyers or tenants to navigate through a property themselves and explore the relationship between spaces.** It is especially effective when layout, circulation and spatial coherence are important decision questions. The tour does not replace an honest description, professional photography, map, technical inspection or physical visit. Before the capture, determine what purpose it serves, which areas may be public, who manages hosting and whether the measurements provided are only exploratory or demonstrably suitable for a technical purpose.
</LeadIntro>

A home seeker wants to know whether the kitchen is directly adjacent to the living space. A prospective tenant wants to understand the walking route from entrance to office or warehouse. An investor may view several properties remotely and want to quickly determine which ones are worth a visit. Single images can partially answer those questions; a navigable tour makes the connection between points of view visible.

That advantage does not arise from scanning every room. A strong real estate tour has a clear starting position, logical route, up-to-date information and a normal contact option outside the viewer.

<QuoteBlock author="Team VisualVibe" role="Creative media agency">
A real estate tour is successful when a candidate understands the layout better and can decide more specifically what the next step is, not when there are just many scan points.
</QuoteBlock>

## What is a 3D real estate tour?

A 3D tour for real estate usually consists of linked panoramic views, often supplemented with depth information and a spatial model. The visitor moves through rooms, rotates the view and can open a map, floor or dollhouse view depending on the platform. Labels, information points and a guided route can provide additional context.

Not every virtual tour contains the same output. A series of 360-degree photos can look similar without a full 3D model or reliable measurement layer. In [what is a 3D tour](/en/kennisbank/3d-vr/what-is-a-3d-tour/) you can read how a photo series, 360 tour, digital twin, video and technical scan differ.

Our [real estate 3D tour service](/en/services/3d-vr-ar/real-estate-3d-tour/) focuses on navigable property presentation. Ask about spaces, functions, hosting duration, delivered files, usage rights and technical limitations.

## For which real estate is a virtual tour useful?

A tour mainly adds information to properties where the mutual location of spaces is important: multi-storey homes, offices with different zones, commercial properties, holiday homes, new-build models and real estate that candidates find difficult to visit spontaneously. Independent exploration can also be a useful first step in case of relocation or international interest.

It is less obvious in a small studio that already fits in a few images, a building that changes quickly during renovation or a file with sensitive areas that are not allowed to become public. An empty or poorly prepared space does not become more attractive by registering it fully interactively. Sometimes a good map and six strong photos make it easier to answer the question.

American research provides context, not a Belgian guarantee. In the [Zillow Consumer Housing Trends Report 2025](https://www.zillow.com/research/prospective-buyers-consumer-housing-trends-2025-35888/), twenty percent of prospective buyers surveyed put a 3D or virtual tour at the top of the listing features. The [NAR Report 2025](https://cms.nar.realtor/sites/default/files/2025-03/2025-home-buyers-and-sellers-generational-trends-report-04-01-2025.pdf) also calls virtual tours useful for some American buyers. Do not generalize this to Belgium and do not promise faster sales.

Each real estate format answers a different question. Photography selects atmosphere and detail, a map abstracts the layout, video drives the story, a drone shows the outside context and a 3D tour lets the visitor navigate themselves.

## Start with the candidate's decision question

Before recording, make a note of what questions you want the tour to answer. What is the route between the living space and the garden? Which floor contains the bedrooms? How does someone reach the reception area? Which areas are not part of the rented part?

Then choose one primary target group. A family assesses different details than an entrepreneur or investor. The same capture can serve multiple visitors, but starting position, labels and text must make one initial route clear.

<NoticeBox variant="info" title="Orientation is not the same as deciding">
A tour can help a candidate ask more specific questions and prepare for a visit. It does not prove any structural condition, legal surface area, energy performance, acoustics or hidden defect. Never place official documents and professional advice behind an interactive impression.
</NoticeBox>

## Prepare the property for a fair capture

A 3D tour sees more directions than one carefully framed photo. Cables, personal documents, family photos, keys, medication, alarm codes, license plates and reflections can still become visible from a different point of view. Therefore, walk the entire route before the operator starts.

Clean and tidy, but do not alter the property in a way that misleads expectations. Keep doors open or closed consistently, repair broken lighting and choose a logical window and curtain position. Remove temporary clutter and personal data. Determine which storage room, technical room, residents' zone or common area is out of scope.

Spatial recognition remains necessary for vacant real estate. Explicitly present virtual staging as a visualization and keep the real state available; added interior may not pass as existing finish.

## Design a route that naturally runs through the building

The best route is not necessarily the shortest. A visitor should be able to understand where they are from a recognizable entrance, without jumping through furniture or landing unexpectedly in a private room. Plan enough consecutive positions to logically connect doors, corners, stairs and level differences.

Glass, mirrors, evenly light walls, outside sun and moving objects can make processing more difficult. The operator therefore checks overlap and alignment during or shortly after capture. People and pets moving between successive viewpoints may appear double or partial. Preferably plan the route without normal habitation or public activities.

Choose the image that provides orientation as a starting position. Use labels only for information that remains current; temporary price, availability and promotion are better displayed on the regular real estate page.

## Separate marketing capture and technical measurement

A marketing tour is created to present a property and support spatial orientation. An engineering recording is designed and validated for a specified measurement, design, documentation or construction workflow. The same equipment can support both types of output in some jobs, but this does not happen automatically.

For technical use, ask which device is being used, what accuracy the manufacturer specifies for that configuration, what conditions apply and what export format is supplied. For example, the official [Matterport Pro3 specifications](https://go.matterport.com/pro3) mention a device-related depth accuracy of plus or minus twenty millimeters at ten meters. That figure does not mean that every measure in every tour, viewer or derived file has the same certainty. Registration, environment, capture distance, processing and desired application remain relevant.

The current [Matterport Terms of Use](https://matterport.com/terms-of-use) also contain general restrictions regarding accuracy, reliability and results to be achieved. Therefore, do not use an interactive measuring tool as a substitute for a surveyor, architect, engineer, site surveyor or other qualified professional when decisions require precise or legally useful measurements.

<NoticeBox variant="danger" title="A measurement icon does not make a tour a bill of quantities">
Indicate in quotation and delivery whether dimensions are indicative, platform generated or validated for a specific technical purpose. Do not order customization, do not assess safety or confirm legal surface based on a marketing viewer without appropriate verification.
</NoticeBox>

## Protect residents, neighbors and sensitive information

A real estate tour can capture recognizable people, family photos, properties, documents, screens, vehicle plates and views of neighbors. As soon as someone is identifiable, data protection and image rights become relevant. The [AVG/GDPR](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj) requires, among other things, purpose limitation, transparency, minimal data processing and appropriate security.

The Belgian [Data Protection Authority](https://www.gegevensbeschermingsautoriteit.be/burger/thema-s/recht-op-afbeelding/wet-van-30-juli-2018) clarifies that images of goods can become personal data when a person can be easily identified with them. So assess not only visible faces, but also combinations of address, device, name, document and other context.

Plan privacy before capture, exclude unnecessary zones and control every navigation point. Auto facial blur may miss photos, text, plates, or security details. The owner of Matterport publishes a [CoStar Global Privacy Notice](https://www.costar.com/about/privacy-notice); so record where data ends up, what agreements apply and who has access.

## Arrange hosting, ownership and post-file deletion

A cloud tour usually only remains active as long as the platform account and appropriate subscription exist. The current [Matterport plans](https://matterport.com/plans) distinguish functions and numbers of active Spaces. Treat hosting as a recurring part of the job, not as an invisible detail in the recording price.

Record who manages the account, who pays annually, who can customize labels and how a tour is transferred. Also agree on when it will go offline: after sale, after signing the lease, after a fixed retention period or when the situation changes significantly. A publicly available tour of an occupied home may continue to show unnecessary privacy and security information after completion.

Record which source and derivative files are delivered and what happens if the platform or subscription is terminated. Photos, map and technical exports are not automatically included in every tour.

## Integrate the tour on a standalone real estate page

An iframe is not a complete real estate page. Above the tour, write a brief description of the building, location, layout and purpose of the tour. Place price, availability, EPC information, documents and contact outside the interactive viewer, so that that information remains findable and usable when the embed does not load.

Use a clear poster image or start button and only load the viewer when this is technically and permission-wise appropriate. Test mobile, slow connection, orientation change and back navigation. Also place the most important action after the tour: request a viewing, download the file or ask a specific question.

Plan text, images, tour and CTA from the web design. The [complete website guide](/en/kennisbank/webdesign/complete-guide-to-building-a-website-in-limburg/) helps discuss architecture, performance and management in a timely manner.

## Provide an accessible alternative to the interactive viewer

Not every visitor can operate a spatial viewer with mouse, touch or movement. An iframe can cause focus problems and the information in a tour is not automatically available to a screen reader. The [WCAG 2.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/) requires, among other things, text alternatives, keyboard controls and the prevention of a keyboard trap.

Therefore, in addition to the tour, offer a textual space summary, logical photo series, readable map and regular contact route. Give the iframe a descriptive title and test whether keyboard focus can be entered and removed. Essential features should not be available solely through a hotspot, color, position, or gesture.

An accessible alternative is not an inferior copy of every camera angle. It must support the same core task: understanding what spaces there are, how they are broadly connected, what the property offers and how someone can request a viewing or information.

## Combine tour, real estate photos and drone images in a targeted manner

Start the real estate page with media that quickly answers the core question. Strong photography gives a controlled first impression. The tour then offers free exploration. A floor plan summarizes the layout. Drone images are relevant when location, plot, parking, roof or outdoor space really play a role.

Avoid repetition and plan the images together so that color, styling and condition match. On different shooting days, check whether furniture, construction phase and outdoor context do not conflict.

## Measure usage without making a sales claim

Link measurement to the chosen task. If you want to support pre-selection, view tour starts, zones visited, use of the map and questions before a viewing. If you want to encourage appointments, measure the route from real estate page to application and the quality of the information entered. A lower number of appointments can mean both better pre-selection and less interest; numbers require context.

Where possible, compare similar files, but recognize differences in price, location, property type, market and media package. In addition, ask what information became clearer and what questions remained.

## Which choices determine planning and price?

Price does not only follow surface area. Floors, route, glass, outdoor points, accessibility, residents, styling, privacy editing, labels, exports, integration and hosting duration play a role. An empty apartment requires a different process than an active commercial property.

Request a quote on output and responsibility: spaces, functions, correction rounds, account, duration and alternative media. Also arrange who manages hosting, corrections and deletion.

Bundling [real estate photography](/en/services/fotografie/real-estate-photography/) with [real estate drone footage](/en/services/drone-fpv/real-estate-drone-footage/) can be practical if light, weather, permission and preparation fit into one schedule. It does not automatically save money when the ideal conditions or specialists differ.

## From interactive impression to useful real estate information

A good real estate tour starts with a candidate question and ends with a managed next step. First determine whether free spatial exploration really adds anything. Then carefully prepare the property, route and privacy, record hosting and removal and build the tour into a standalone page with text, photos, map and contact.

Keep the distinction clear: a marketing tour helps someone understand a space; technical dimensions require a specified and validated workflow.
