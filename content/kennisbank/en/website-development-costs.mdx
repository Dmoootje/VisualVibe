# How much does a website cost in Belgium?

<LeadIntro>
Having a website built in Belgium can cost a few hundred euros or tens of thousands. Across five public Belgian pricing examples checked on 10 July 2026, advertised prices ranged from <strong>€345 excluding VAT for a tightly defined template one-pager</strong> to <strong>€20,000 or more for extensive custom work</strong>. This is a snapshot of selected offers, not a representative market study, official average or VisualVibe price list. The useful question is therefore not only what a website costs, but <strong>which strategy, content, features and ongoing support the price includes</strong>.
</LeadIntro>

Perhaps you have already received two quotations that seem impossible to compare. One lists five pages, hosting and a contact form. The other includes research, copywriting, photography, search engine optimisation, custom design and aftercare. Both are described as websites, but in practice they are two very different projects.

This guide will help you set a realistic budget and compare quotations on equal terms. For the complete process, also read our [guide to building a website in Limburg](/en/knowledge-base/web-design/complete-guide-to-building-a-website-in-limburg/).

## How much does a website cost in Belgium in 2026?

There is no official Belgian statistic for the average cost of a website. A single average without a defined scope has little value, because content, quality, functionality and responsibilities can differ substantially.

For a useful starting point, we compared five public Belgian pricing pages from template providers, freelancers and agencies: [WebsiteDeal](https://websitedeal.be/), [Egidex](https://egidex.be/website-laten-maken/), [Upshift](https://upshift.be/expertise/website-laten-maken), [OnlineGedoe](https://www.onlinegedoe.be/prijzen) and [Conversal](https://www.conversal.be/faq/hoeveel-kost-een-website/). Each linked a public price or price range to a described package or project type on the review date.

Those five examples suggest the indicative ranges below. They were not selected at random and do not represent the Belgian market as a whole.

A low-cost offer is not automatically a poor one. A template one-pager may be exactly right for a new business with one service and a clear contact action. Conversely, an expensive project is not automatically good. Price only becomes meaningful when the approach fits what your business needs. Compare the substance of a [one-page site and a full website](/en/knowledge-base/web-design/one-page-site-or-full-website/) before comparing their totals.

## Why does one website cost €500 and another €15,000?

The difference is rarely the number of buttons or the colour of the homepage. It lies in the thinking, original content, specialist work and responsibility involved.

Consider a commercial space. An empty unit with standard furniture can be ready quickly. A space designed around your brand, visitor flows, technology and daily operations takes more planning and specialist input. Both may be useful, but they do not solve the same problem.

The same distinction applies to websites:

- A **template product** reuses an existing structure and limits the choices, making delivery quicker and less expensive.
- A **professional business website** translates your offer and audience into a dedicated page structure, visual identity and contact flow.
- A **growth website** is prepared for several services, sectors, regions, case studies and knowledge content.
- An **online shop or platform** contains business logic for products, payments, shipping, accounts, bookings, stock or integrations.

Five pages therefore tell you surprisingly little. Five unique pages involving research, photography and custom design may require more work than 50 project pages generated from one well-designed template.

## What makes up the price of a professional website?

A sound quotation shows what happens before, during and after development.

### Strategy and briefing

A website begins with decisions. Who do you want to reach, which problem do you solve, which services take priority and what should a visitor do? Without these answers, a design may look attractive while leaving its commercial purpose to chance.

### Sitemap and information architecture

The sitemap determines which subjects need their own page and how visitors and search engines understand the relationships between them. For an SME, that may include services, sub-services, sectors, regions, case studies and a knowledge base. This is also the stage at which you prevent overlap and plan for future expansion.

### UX and visual design

UX concerns use. Can someone quickly find what they need and confidently contact, book or buy? Visual design then translates your brand into typography, colour, imagery, components and mobile layouts. Adapting an existing theme takes less work than developing a unique design system.

### Copywriting and content migration

Copy is often underestimated. Reusing existing text appears free, but old content rarely fits a new structure exactly. Professional copywriting covers interviews, search intent, content architecture, editing, metadata and calls to action. Safely migrating blog posts, products or case studies also takes time.

### Photography, video and graphic content

Stock images can provide a temporary solution, but original media shows your team, process and results. Because VisualVibe combines web design, [corporate photography](/en/services/photography/corporate-photography/) and video, image ratios and shoots can be planned during design. Media then becomes part of the story rather than decoration inserted into empty spaces later.

### Development and CMS

During development, designs become functioning components, forms, content fields and integrations. A [WordPress website](/en/services/web-design/wordpress-website/) can be useful when you want to manage a large amount of content yourself. Custom technology may make more sense when speed, complex data flows or scalability matter more. WordPress itself is [open-source software](https://wordpress.org/about/features/), but design, configuration, hosting, extensions and maintenance still require professional work.

### SEO foundations, performance and accessibility

Basic SEO involves more than a few keywords. It includes crawlable pages, logical URLs, metadata, headings, internal links, redirects, structured data and a sound mobile experience. For a good user experience, Google recommends an LCP within 2.5 seconds, INP below 200 milliseconds and CLS below 0.1. These [Core Web Vitals](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/core-web-vitals) are useful quality targets, but do not guarantee a high ranking. Read how [website speed and SEO are connected](/en/knowledge-base/web-design/why-a-faster-website-ranks-better/) before reducing performance to one score or ranking factor.

Use the [technical SEO checklist](/en/knowledge-base/seo-geo/technical-seo-checklist/) to assess what a quoted SEO foundation should actually cover.

Accessibility also requires deliberate decisions about contrast, keyboard operation, forms, focus, labels and alternative text. Additional [European accessibility rules](https://europa.eu/youreurope/business/selling-in-eu/selling-goods-services/accessibility/index_en.htm) may apply to certain consumer services, including e-commerce, since 28 June 2025. Microenterprises providing services may be exempt under certain conditions. Have the rules assessed for your specific situation and treat accessibility as a quality standard in any event.

### Testing, launch and handover

Forms, navigation, mobile behaviour, redirects, tracking, cookies, performance and error pages should all be tested before launch. Domain settings, indexing, training and documentation follow. A low quotation that excludes these steps usually shifts the risk to the customer.

## The factors with the greatest effect on your quotation

### 1. The number of unique page types

A homepage, service page, case study, blog article and product detail page each need a different structure. The number of unique templates affects the price more than the raw number of URLs.

### 2. Template or custom design

A template limits design time and decisions. Custom work allows for a more distinctive experience, but requires research, design and additional testing. The right choice depends on your need to stand out, not prestige.

### 3. Availability of copy and images

Are your texts current, accurate and suited to the new sitemap? Will the photographs work on large screens and mobile devices? Content that still needs to be created, selected or rewritten belongs in the schedule and quotation.

### 4. Functionality

A simple form is different from a calculator, booking system, customer portal or product configurator. Every feature requires design, error handling, security, testing and often maintenance.

### 5. External integrations

CRM, email marketing, accounting, stock, bookings and payment providers each have their own APIs and conditions. A standard integration may be quick. Custom integration and two-way synchronisation require substantially more work.

### 6. Multiple languages

A multilingual website needs more than a translation button. Every language requires its own content, metadata, URLs, navigation and quality assurance. Automatic translation without editing can damage your brand and make the copy less clear.

### 7. Online shop logic

Building an [online shop](/en/services/web-design/online-shop-development/) involves more than product cards. It may include variants, VAT, payments, shipping, stock, returns, emails and statutory information. The [FPS Economy](https://economie.fgov.be/sites/default/files/Files/Entreprises/guidelines-informatieverplichtingen-in-het-kader-van-e-commerce.pdf) explains which information consumers must receive before and during a purchase. Our [complete SME guide to online shops](/en/knowledge-base/web-design/online-shop-development-for-smes/) puts these decisions into a practical project sequence.

### 8. Migration and protecting search visibility

For an existing website, old URLs, valuable content and inbound links need to be mapped. A redesign without a redirect plan can waste established visibility. Including [SEO during website development](/en/services/web-design/seo-website/) is usually more efficient than repairing the structure later. First assess [when a redesign is genuinely necessary](/en/knowledge-base/web-design/when-to-redesign-your-website/) and what can be retained safely.

### 9. Privacy, cookies and analytics

Not every site needs the same consent solution. Strictly necessary cookies are different from analytics, advertising pixels and marketing embeds. The [Belgian Data Protection Authority](https://www.gegevensbeschermingsautoriteit.be/cookies-en-andere-traceringsmiddelen) explains that non-essential cookies require prior, freely given and specific consent. Correct implementation requires an inventory, appropriate technology and clear information, not merely a standard banner.

### 10. Guidance and aftercare

Project management, feedback rounds, training, documentation and support are real work. A quotation without guidance may look cheaper, but demands more time and technical knowledge from you.

### Domain name

A `.be` domain is registered through a registrar. DNS Belgium currently charges registrars €5.50 excluding VAT for registration or annual renewal. This is a wholesale price, not necessarily your final price. The registrar may bundle hosting, mailboxes and support. Check the renewal price and ensure that your business is registered as the domain holder.

### Hosting

Hosting prices vary widely depending on capacity, management, backups, support and availability. A low introductory price says little about renewal or technical assistance. Ask what performance is included and who responds when the site is unavailable.

### Maintenance and security

Software, dependencies and integrations change. Professional [website maintenance](/en/services/web-design/website-maintenance/) can include updates, monitoring, backups and minor changes. Specify exactly what maintenance covers and what will be billed as new work.

### Content and growth

A website is not automatically finished at launch. New case studies, services, images and knowledge articles keep it current. Ongoing SEO or content production is a separate growth programme and should not be hidden beneath a vague promise of "SEO included".

## How do you calculate the total cost over three years?

Do not look only at the initial invoice. Apply the same simple calculation to every quotation:

```txt
total cost over 3 years =
development and launch
+ domain and hosting for 3 years
+ maintenance and licences for 3 years
+ expected content and expansions
+ internal time for coordination and management
```

A subscription of €150 per month may look low beside a project fee. Over 36 months, it totals €5,400 before any setup, content or change fees. Conversely, a one-off development price without maintenance may lead to unexpected work later. Neither model is automatically better. Transparency and appropriate ownership matter more.

Compare prices consistently excluding or including VAT. According to [FPS Finance, the standard Belgian rate is 21%](https://financien.belgium.be/nl/ondernemingen/btw/btw-plicht/tarieven-en-berekening/btw-tarieven). A quotation of €5,000 excluding VAT therefore totals €6,050 including VAT. Whether your business can recover VAT, and how much, depends on its tax position.

## WordPress, website builder or custom development: what are you really paying for?

### Do it yourself or use a website builder

A builder can be suitable when you have time, the scope is small and you need few unique features. The software price may be low, but your hours spent on structure, copy, design, privacy, SEO and testing do not disappear. Also consider export options, platform charges and what happens when you want to move.

### WordPress

WordPress offers flexibility and a large ecosystem. It can be cost-effective when existing components suit the requirement. Quality still depends on the theme, extensions, configuration, performance, security and maintenance. "WordPress is free" is not a useful project budget.

### Custom development

Custom work makes sense when standard solutions do not fit your brand, processes or integrations. You pay more for analysis and development, but may avoid restrictions or subscriptions that obstruct growth. A professional website does not automatically require custom technology. It should solve a demonstrable problem.

## Do it yourself, hire a freelancer or work with an agency?

This decision affects not only price, but also who is responsible for strategy, content, media, technology and continuity.

- **Do it yourself:** lowest external cost, greatest demand on your own time and expertise.
- **Template provider:** fast, clearly bounded delivery with less freedom and guidance.
- **Freelancer:** direct communication and often deep specialisation, but you may need additional partners for copy, media or complex technology.
- **Multidisciplinary agency:** several disciplines in one project, usually with more project guidance and a broader total scope.

An hourly rate rarely explains a quotation in full. Web designers, developers, copywriters and media professionals may charge by the hour, day, phase or project. Compare the expertise, deliverables, decisions and responsibilities included in the total.

## When is a low-cost website a sensible choice?

A low-cost website can be a rational choice when:

- you offer one clear service or are running a temporary campaign;
- your brand and content are ready;
- you do not need complex integrations;
- you expect little organic traffic for the time being;
- you understand and accept the platform's limitations;
- you deliberately prioritise speed over differentiation.

A [one-page website](/en/services/web-design/one-page-website/) may then be stronger than a half-complete site with seven thin pages.

Low cost often becomes expensive when you need to migrate again soon after launch, lack access to essential accounts, cannot expand the technology or discover that important content and redirects are missing. The cost then includes not only a second development project, but lost time, duplicated content work and potential damage to existing search visibility.

## How does VisualVibe define the scope of a website?

A reliable cost estimate does not start with a package name. It starts with what the website needs to do for your business. This is particularly true of [web design for SMEs](/en/sectors/smes/), where a compact contact site, an extensive service website and an integrated sales platform each require a different investment.

The detailed [new website checklist](/en/knowledge-base/web-design/new-website-checklist/) helps you collect the information a supplier needs to define scope, responsibilities and price accurately.

To receive an accurate estimate, collect this information first:

- the primary business objective;
- target audiences and regions;
- services or products;
- desired actions, such as calling, booking, buying or requesting a quotation;
- the existing website, copy, photographs and data;
- examples of websites you do and do not like;
- required languages and integrations;
- who will manage the website after launch;
- desired timing and a realistic budget range.

Sharing a budget range is not an invitation to raise the price automatically to that ceiling. It helps everyone discuss a suitable solution from the outset and avoid unrealistic proposals.
