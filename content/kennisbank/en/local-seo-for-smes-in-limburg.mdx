<LeadIntro>

## What is local SEO?

Local SEO is optimizing your online presence so that you are better found by people in a specific region. This can be done via Google Search, Google Maps, your Google Business Profile, local landing pages, reviews, photos, company data, content and technical SEO.

Google explains that information in a Business Profile can come from multiple sources, including the business itself, public web content, and user contributions. Your website and profile do not have to be identical, but they must clearly and correctly describe the same company, offer and work area. Learn how [Google collects and uses business information](https://support.google.com/business/answer/2721884?hl=en).

When someone searches for a service with a local intent, your profile, website, and evidence should come together to provide a logical answer. A place name in the H1 does not in itself make a page locally relevant.

A common mistake is that companies have one general services page that lists everything. For example:

```txt
Our services:
- Web design
-SEO
- Photography
- Video
-Drone
```

That's useful for visitors who already know you, but weak for SEO. Someone who searches for 'company video Limburg' has a different intention than someone who searches for 'have a website made in Limburg'. Both searches deserve a different page.

Therefore, this structure is stronger:

```txt
/en/services/web-design/
/en/services/seo/
/en/services/photography/
/en/services/videography/
/en/services/drone-fpv/
/en/regio/limburg-belgium/
```

This way, each page can answer one specific search intent.

The [Google SEO Starter Guide](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/seo-starter-guide) recommends logical site organization, descriptive URLs, and understandable internal links. For local SEO, this does not mean that every place needs a page, but that every published route must have a clear function and sufficient content of its own.

<NoticeBox variant="warning" title="Build only a local subpage with unique value">
A dedicated Limburg web-design subpage only makes sense once you have your own local context, relevant evidence and a clear search intent. Without that foundation, one strong [regional page for Limburg](/regio/limburg/) is more valuable than several thin variants.
</NoticeBox>

## Google Business Profile: local visibility outside your website

For local SEO, your Google Business Profile has its own role in addition to the website. The profile can appear in Google Maps and local search results and gives customers quick access to categories, location or service area, hours, contact information, photos and reviews, among other things.

Always use the real company name and correct location or service area information. The [Corporate Representation Guidelines](https://support.google.com/business/answer/3038177?hl=en) warns against unnecessary additions to the company name, while the [Category Manual](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7249669?hl=en) advises choosing the most specific appropriate main category.

A strong profile combines correct basic data with current evidence:

      "Complete description of the company",
      "Services corresponding to the website",
    ]}
  />
  <ChecklistBlock
    title="Current evidence"
    className="my-0 h-full"
    items={[
      "Real photos and relevant videos",
      "Recent and honest reviews",
      "Helpful answers to reviews",
      "Updates or posts when they add something",
      "Answers to recurring customer questions",
      "A verified and actively managed profile",
    ]}
  />
</FeatureGrid>

<NoticeBox variant="info" title="How Google organizes local results">
According to Google, local results are mainly based on **relevance, distance and popularity**. Complete company information helps Google understand your offering; You cannot optimize distance and prominence grows through mentions, links and reviews, among other things. Check out the [Official Local Ranking Tips](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7091?hl=nl).
</NoticeBox>

It is important for SMEs that profile and website do not contradict each other. Your company name, telephone number, address or service area, hours and services must be current. A complete profile helps customers assess your offer and gives Google more correct information to make a relevant match.

Photos also make a difference. A Google Business Profile with real company photos, team images, project photos or atmospheric images feels much more reliable than an empty profile. That is why local SEO fits well with professional photography.

**Practical tip:** Do not just use logos or stock images on your Google Business Profile, but use real photos of your work, team, location, achievements or atmosphere. This allows a customer to better assess what they can expect.

For full operational control, go through the [Google Business Profile Checklist](/en/kennisbank/seo-geo/optimise-google-business-profile/), with categories, service area, hours, services, photos, reviews and monthly follow-up.

## Regional pages: useful or dangerous?

Regional pages can be useful when answering a question of your own. The problem arises with location pages with the same text and only a different place name. They hardly help the visitor and may fall under [Google's policy against abuse with scaled content](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies#scaled-content) if they are mainly produced to manipulate search results.

<DoDontGrid
  doTitle="Valuable region page"
  dontTitle="Thin Location Page"
  dos={[
    "Has a unique intro and real local context",
    "Links relevant services to the needs in the region",
    "Shows local cases, realizations or recognizable examples",
    "Logically links to services, knowledge content and contact",
    "Answers questions that arise for local customers",
    "Uses proprietary metadata and descriptive image context",
    "Has a clear search intent and next step",
  ]}
  donts={[
    "Copy the same text to each city",
    "Only replaces the city name in title and paragraphs",
    "does not show local context, evidence or cases",
    "Only exists to use another keyword",
    "Pushes straight to contact without answering the question",
    "Competes with other pages for the same search intent",
    "Promises local relevance without demonstrating it",
  ]}
/>

A broad [regional page for Limburg](/regio/limburg/) can explain which SMEs you help, how services come together and what local evidence you have. More specific pages for Bilzen-Hoeselt or Tongeren-Borgloon are only useful if they add their own demonstrable layer.

The [Google Images Guidelines](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/google-images) emphasizes relevant text context and descriptive alt text. The [W3C image tutorial](https://www.w3.org/WAI/tutorials/images/) also distinguishes between informative, functional and decorative images; for example, a decorative image does not need to have descriptive alt text.

Bad:

```txt
IMG_4837.jpg
alt="photo"
```

Good:

```txt
visualvibe-team-bedrijfsshoot-limburg.webp
alt="The VisualVibe team during a corporate shoot in Limburg"
```

That is better for accessibility, recognisability and context. Anyone who already includes images during website planning can also have [photography](/en/services/fotografie/) specifically linked to services, cases and local landing pages.
