<LeadIntro>
**GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimisation:** preparing your website so generative systems can retrieve relevant pages, understand their information and potentially cite them as sources. GEO builds on SEO, but adds a focus on retrieval, source quality, citeable claims, entities and platform access. It is not a formula for buying or forcing a citation. Every platform decides which sources suit a question and how they appear in an answer.
</LeadIntro>

Traditional search results usually present a list of links. A generative system may first run several searches, retrieve information from different pages and turn it into a summary with supporting links. Your page therefore needs to do more than rank for one keyword. It must also address a relevant sub-question, remain technically available and contain sufficiently clear evidence to be used responsibly.

The broader foundations are covered in our pillar guide to [appearing in Google, ChatGPT and AI search results](/en/kennisbank/seo-geo/how-to-appear-in-ai-search-results-geo-aeo/). This article focuses solely on GEO: how retrieval and citations work, what makes a source trustworthy, which controls platforms offer and how to measure results without false certainty.

## Where did the term GEO come from?

The term was developed academically in the paper [GEO: Generative Engine Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735), originally submitted in November 2023 and later accepted at KDD 2024. The researchers described generative engines as systems that collect and summarise information from multiple sources. They also introduced a benchmark for evaluating source visibility in generated answers.

Within that experimental setup, the paper reported improvements of up to 40 percent for certain optimisations and domains. That figure is not a general promise for Google, ChatGPT, Copilot or an SME website. The result came from a specific benchmark, the strategies did not perform identically in every domain, and the platforms have changed significantly since then.

<NoticeBox variant="warning" title="Use research as insight, not a sales promise">
A benchmark result does not prove that the same change will have the same effect on every platform today. Test principles such as clear sourcing and relevant statistics, but do not present a fixed GEO percentage or guaranteed citation.
</NoticeBox>

The precise systems depend on the platform and are not fully public. Four practical stages can nevertheless help explain the process:

In [AI features and your website](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/ai-features), Google explains that AI Overviews and AI Mode can use query fan-out. To be eligible as a supporting link, a page must be indexed and permitted to show a snippet in Google Search. There are no additional technical AI requirements, and inclusion is never guaranteed.

This also makes a [technical SEO checklist](/en/kennisbank/seo-geo/technical-seo-checklist/) relevant to GEO. An excellent study blocked by robots.txt, noindex, an incorrect canonical or a scripting problem is difficult to retrieve as a normal web source.

## What makes content citeable?

Citeability is not a separate HTML element. A passage is useful primarily when someone can establish exactly what is being claimed, in which context and on what basis.

For example, an original case is stronger when it does more than call a project "successful". State the starting point, approach, period, limitations and measured outcome. A price comparison is more useful when VAT, scope and the reference date are visible. A local claim gains meaning through real case studies and accurate business information, as explained in [local SEO for SMEs in Limburg](/en/kennisbank/seo-geo/local-seo-for-smes-in-limburg/).

Google's guidance on [helpful, reliable content](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content) asks for elements including clear expertise, sources and content made for people. That is not a secret GEO factor, but it is a sound quality foundation for any source.

## Entities and consistency without keyword stuffing

A generative system needs to distinguish who or what is being discussed. Use consistent official names for your organisation, services, products, authors and working areas. Explain an abbreviation on first use, and do not alternate unnecessarily between five names for the same offer.

For VisualVibe, this means that "AI SEO, AEO and GEO" retains the same meaning wherever the service is relevant. Regions such as Limburg or Antwerp appear only when the content and service genuinely relate to them. A page about [SEO for websites in Limburg](/en/kennisbank/seo-geo/seo-for-websites-in-limburg/) needs local context; a general definition of GEO does not need to repeat "Limburg" in every paragraph.

Structured data can provide explicit information about visible entities and page types. Google's [introduction to structured data](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/intro-structured-data) says that markup must match what users can see on the page. Google also says no special schema is required for AI Overviews or AI Mode. Schema can support SEO and potential search features, but it is not an admission ticket to generative citations.

## Platform controls: search, training and user actions differ

An important part of GEO is deliberately deciding which systems may retrieve your content. The names and effects vary by platform.

The current [OpenAI crawler documentation](https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/bots) explicitly says that OAI-SearchBot and GPTBot can be allowed or blocked independently. A publisher can permit search visibility while stating that its content may not be crawled for model training. OpenAI notes that processing a Search robots change may take about 24 hours.

The [OpenAI Publishers FAQ](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/12627856-publishers-and-developers-faq) adds that a public website may appear in ChatGPT Search and that OAI-SearchBot requires access for content to appear in summaries and snippets. To exclude even a title and link, publishers need to apply noindex correctly and allow the crawler to read that meta instruction.

<NoticeBox variant="info" title="A robots decision is also a policy decision">
Do not change crawler rules blindly for an SEO test. Decide with technical, marketing, privacy and, where appropriate, legal colleagues which search, training and user scenarios suit your organisation.
</NoticeBox>

## You do not need special AI files or writing codes

Google says its AI features require no special machine-readable AI files or extra schema. An llms.txt file is therefore not required to appear in Google AI Overviews or AI Mode. Other services may make their own choices, but that does not make such a file a universal standard or ranking factor.

Nor is "chunking" an instruction to make every paragraph unnaturally short. Passages should make sense independently, but the complete page must retain relationships, nuance and evidence. Write for a person who needs to understand the decision, then make the structure technically accessible.

## How do you measure GEO without false certainty?

GEO measurement varies by platform and remains incomplete. Combine the information that is available with your own analytics.

In February 2026, Bing launched [AI Performance in Bing Webmaster Tools](https://blogs.bing.com/webmaster/February-2026/Introducing-AI-Performance-in-Bing-Webmaster-Tools-Public-Preview). The report shows:

- total citations in supported generative answers;
- the average number of unique cited pages per day;
- a sample of grounding queries;
- citation activity by URL;
- trends over the selected period.

Bing stresses that these figures do not reveal the position, authority or role a page had within one specific answer. Use them to identify patterns and opportunities for improvement, not to calculate a universal GEO score.

Google is rolling out a separate [Generative AI performance report](https://support.google.com/webmasters/answer/16984139) to a subset of Search Console properties. It reports generative impressions with breakdowns including page, country and device. Properties without the report continue to use general web performance data. Neither system isolates every individual source citation within a single answer. OpenAI notes that referrals from ChatGPT Search may include the `utm_source=chatgpt.com` parameter. Analyse those visits, but remember that no dashboard covers the complete generative landscape.

## A practical GEO plan for an SME

Do not begin by rewriting a hundred pages. Choose one commercially important topic and put its source chain in order.

1. Identify which page is the canonical source for the topic.
2. Test crawling, indexing, canonicals and snippet permissions.
3. Mark claims that lack a date, method, example or primary source.
4. Add genuine experience through cases, photographs, process knowledge or original data.
5. Establish relationships with services, authors, regions and follow-up topics.
6. Deliberately review OAI-SearchBot, GPTBot and other relevant platform controls.
7. Record a baseline and follow citations, referrals and conversions over time.

For a local SME, this may begin with a well-managed [Google Business Profile](/en/kennisbank/seo-geo/optimise-google-business-profile/), a consistent service structure and evidence from genuine projects. [SEO content for service businesses](/en/kennisbank/seo-geo/seo-content-for-service-businesses/) helps turn operational expertise into pages that contain more than generic marketing claims.

On a new website, source structure and technology should be considered together from the start. The [complete guide to building a website](/en/kennisbank/webdesign/complete-guide-to-building-a-website-in-limburg/) places this work within the full project. The [new website checklist](/en/kennisbank/webdesign/new-website-checklist/) prevents authorship, metadata, analytics and internal links from becoming afterthoughts. Good [website speed](/en/kennisbank/webdesign/why-a-fast-website-can-rank-better/) remains part of a usable technical foundation, but it is not a GEO guarantee on its own.

## Conclusion: become a better source, not a louder page

GEO is not a hunt for a hidden prompt formula. It brings together accessible technology, reliable information, clear entities, evidence and deliberate platform choices. Doing this well creates a stronger website for people while enabling responsible retrieval and citation.
